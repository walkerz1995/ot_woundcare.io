Introduction: Why Wound Care Matters

Wounds significantly impact occupational performance across mobility, self-care, rest, and participation. OT addresses functional factors including mobility, positioning, habits, and cognition that contribute to wound development.

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Identify three main wound types in SNF settings
  • Describe OT-specific interventions for prevention
  • Recognize key risk factors
  • Apply evidence-based strategies

Research shows that interdisciplinary teamwork with OT central to mobility and ADL interventions leads to better outcomes and fewer rehospitalizations. Quality improvement requires consistent, system-level programs.

Critical Risk Factors

üö´ Immobility

Difficulty moving or changing positions due to physical limitations, neurological conditions, cognition, or pain.

OT Approaches

  • Strengthening, ROM, neuromuscular re-education
  • Functional mobility training (transfers)
  • Routines incorporating movement and weight shifting

üíß Incontinence

Urine and stool irritate skin, increasing risk for pressure injuries, skin tears, and infection.

OT Approaches

  • Establish functional toileting routines
  • Train clothing management, hygiene, and safe transfers
  • Recommend adaptive equipment
Module 4 of 5

Prevention Strategies

1. Positioning & Seating

  • Pressure redistribution equipment (tilt-in-space wheelchairs)
  • Pressure-relieving cushions
  • Proper wheelchair adjustments (seat depth, back support)
  • Specialized beds and positioning protocols

2. Mobility & Functional Activity

  • Incorporate movement into meaningful tasks
  • Grade activities to maintain strength
  • Encourage standing tasks and functional ambulation

3. Education & Training

  • Skin inspection and care techniques
  • Transfer training to reduce skin tears
  • Education on protective clothing
  • Cognitive supports (signs, alarms, schedules)
